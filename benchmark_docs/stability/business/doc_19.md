# Smart Agriculture Sensors Business Strategy

1. Executive Summary
The venture builds a focused product line around Smart Agriculture Sensors with a phased commercial rollout.

Business Objectives
- Validate product-market fit in one vertical
- Reach repeatable onboarding and retention
- Build partner channel pipeline

2. Technology Overview
The stack combines edge data capture, cloud analytics, and workflow automation.

Market Opportunity
Target customers are mid-size operators with compliance and reliability pressure.

| Segment | Pain Point | Willingness to Pay |
|---|---|---|
| Enterprise | Downtime risk | High |
| Mid-market | Manual overhead | Medium |
| Public sector | Reporting burden | Medium |

3. Product Strategy
- Start with core workflow automation
- Add premium diagnostics package
- Expand to multi-site orchestration

Revenue Model
Subscription + usage-based metering for high-volume customers.

| Revenue Stream | Pricing Basis | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Platform License | Per workspace | Annual contracts |
| Data Processing | Per 1k events | Tiered overages |
| Services | Fixed engagement | Onboarding and training |

Risk Assessment
Top risks include long procurement cycles and integration complexity.

Legal Considerations
Data handling policy, audit trails, and contractual SLAs are mandatory.

Assumptions:
- Initial launch geography is limited
- First-year focus is product reliability
