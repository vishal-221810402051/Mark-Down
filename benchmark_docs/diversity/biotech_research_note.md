## Context

The original goal was to test whether **soil isolates from agricultural runoff zones** produce antimicrobial peptides active against common gram-negative pathogens.

Initial hypothesis:  
Certain environmental Bacillus or Streptomyces strains may secrete peptide molecules capable of suppressing opportunistic pathogens (E. coli variants used here as lab strains).

This idea came from earlier screening data in an unrelated study, though the conditions used here are not identical.

Also: the soil samples were not sterile when collected. That may complicate interpretation later.

---

## Sample Sources

Samples collected from three locations near irrigation canals.

Locations recorded only approximately.

| Sample ID | Site description | Soil depth | Moisture | Notes |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|----------|------|
| S1 | canal edge | ~5 cm | damp | strong organic smell |
| S2 | irrigation field | ~10 cm | dry | clay heavy |
| S3 | drainage basin | ~3 cm | wet | dark sediment |

Transported to lab in sterile tubes but **temperature not controlled** during transit (~2 hours).

This could affect microbial composition.

## Preparation (informal workflow)

Steps actually followed in the lab:

1. Soil suspended in PBS
2. Vortex mixing (approx 30 seconds)
3. Serial dilution performed (10⁻² to 10⁻⁶)
4. Spread onto nutrient agar plates

However:  
Plate incubation temperature changed from 30°C → 28°C after day 1 due to incubator load.

Not sure if this matters.

## Antimicrobial Screening Attempt

Test organism:  
Lab E. coli strain K12 (non-pathogenic)

Procedure roughly:


Grow isolate culture overnight

Centrifuge culture

Collect supernatant

Apply supernatant to agar plate with E. coli lawn

Observe inhibition zones


Not fully standardized — volumes differed slightly between tests.

---

## Preliminary Observations

Most isolates showed **no clear inhibition**.

But two isolates (from S3) produced faint inhibition zones.

Approximate results:

| Isolate | Zone size (mm) | Reliability |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| S3-B2 | ~3 mm | uncertain |
| S3-D1 | ~5 mm | moderate |
| others | none | consistent |

However plates were unevenly spread so measurements may be inaccurate.

Also: contamination suspected on one plate.

---

## Chemical Extraction Attempt (partial)

A quick extraction was attempted using ethanol precipitation.

Steps written on sticky note:

- centrifuge supernatant
- add ethanol (1:3 ratio)
- incubate cold (4°C) ~2h
- pellet proteins

But the pellet yield was extremely small.

Possibilities:
- compound concentration too low
- antimicrobial not peptide based
- method unsuitable

Needs repeating with better protocol.
