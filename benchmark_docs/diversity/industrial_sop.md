## Purpose

To provide a checklist-driven procedure for:

• Routine conveyor belt inspection  
• Jam response protocol  
• Emergency stoppage handling  
• Restart verification

The goal is to minimize downtime and prevent equipment damage or worker injury.

---

## Scope

Applies to:

- Packaging Line 1
- Packaging Line 2
- Overflow sorting lane

Does NOT apply to robotic sorting cells (covered in SOP-522).

Operators responsible:

- Shift Technician
- Line Supervisor
- Maintenance Technician (if escalation required)

## Safety Requirements (MANDATORY)

Before interacting with any conveyor equipment:

☐ Wear protective gloves  
☐ Wear safety glasses  
☐ Confirm emergency stop button location  
☐ Verify that loose clothing is secured  

Warning:  
Some conveyor sections may continue moving briefly after power cutoff due to momentum.

---

# SECTION 1 — Routine Inspection (Start of Shift)

Frequency: once per shift

### Checklist

☐ Verify belt alignment  
☐ Inspect rollers for debris accumulation  
☐ Check belt tension indicator  
☐ Confirm sensor lights are active  
☐ Inspect side guards

Notes:  
Small debris often accumulates near the secondary roller section. If present, clear before starting the line.

---

### Subsection: Sensor Status

The conveyor system uses optical sensors to detect packages.

Expected status indicators:

| Sensor | Indicator | Normal State |
|------|------|------|
| S1 Entry Sensor | Green LED | Active |
| S2 Mid Sensor | Green LED | Active |
| S3 Exit Sensor | Green LED | Active |

If any sensor shows **red or blinking state**, pause line startup and inspect alignment.

---

# SECTION 2 — Jam Detection

Jams may occur when packages become wedged between rollers or accumulate faster than downstream processing.

Common jam indicators:

• sudden slowdown of belt  
• packages tilting sideways  
• audible motor strain  
• sensor misread events

Operators should visually confirm before stopping the line.

---

# SECTION 3 — Jam Response Procedure

Follow the steps below exactly.

### Immediate Actions

☐ Press **local emergency stop**  
☐ Notify nearby operators  
☐ Confirm belt movement has stopped  
☐ Identify jam location

Do not attempt removal while belt is moving.

---

### Clearing the Jam

Procedure:

1. Remove packages surrounding the jam
2. Identify the blocking object
3. Lift object vertically if possible
4. Avoid pulling against belt motion direction

In some cases two operators may be required.

---

### If Jam Cannot Be Cleared

Escalation checklist:

☐ Notify shift supervisor  
☐ Request maintenance technician  
☐ Lock out power source

Lockout tag must be attached to the motor switch.

---

# SECTION 4 — Restart Procedure

After jam removal:

☐ Confirm conveyor path is clear  
☐ Verify sensors unobstructed  
☐ Confirm belt alignment  
☐ Remove emergency stop lock

Restart sequence:

1. Power system ON
2. Wait for controller initialization (~5 seconds)
3. Start belt at low speed
4. Observe movement for 10 seconds

If abnormal vibration occurs, stop system again.

---

## Subsection: Controller Reset (Occasional)

In rare cases the conveyor controller must be reset.

Steps:


Turn controller OFF

Wait 10 seconds

Turn controller ON

Check system status screen


Controller screen should display:

SYSTEM READY

If error codes appear, maintenance intervention is required.

---

# SECTION 5 — Preventive Maintenance

Weekly maintenance checklist:

☐ Clean roller assemblies  
☐ Inspect drive motor mounts  
☐ Lubricate bearing housings  
☐ Check tension calibration

Record findings in maintenance log.
