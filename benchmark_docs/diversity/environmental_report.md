Environmental Monitoring Report  
River Basin Sediment Quality Survey – Q2 Field Assessment

Prepared for: Regional Water Authority  
Compiled by: Environmental Field Monitoring Team  
Survey window: April–June (year not strictly logged in all entries)  

Note: Data were collected during multiple site visits. Some measurements were taken under different weather conditions, which may slightly affect comparability.

---

## 1. Survey Overview

The purpose of the sediment quality survey was to evaluate pollutant accumulation in the lower river basin, particularly in regions downstream from industrial activity.

The survey targeted three main monitoring zones:

| Zone | Description | Approx. River KM |
|-----|-------------|----------------|
| A | upstream reference area | 5–7 |
| B | mixed agricultural runoff zone | 12–15 |
| C | downstream industrial discharge region | 21–24 |

Sediment cores were extracted using a manual sampling probe. Each core was divided into top and lower layers for analysis.

Some sites were revisited twice due to inconsistent readings during the first sampling.

## 4. Field Observations

Field teams recorded several environmental indicators that may influence sediment conditions.

Observations included:

- increased algae growth in Zone B
- strong chemical odor detected near Site S05
- sediment color changes in deeper layers
- slower water flow during late afternoon sampling

Weather conditions varied between visits.

One sampling day experienced light rainfall, which may have temporarily altered sediment composition.

---

## 5. Comparative Trends

When comparing the upstream and downstream zones, the following patterns were observed:

1. heavy metal concentrations increase progressively downstream  
2. hydrocarbon contamination is minimal upstream but rises significantly near industrial discharge points  
3. sediment density appears higher in zones with slower water flow  

However, only six sites were tested, which limits statistical confidence.

## 7. Notes from Lab Processing

During lab preparation:

- two sediment samples required additional drying
- one mercury reading was repeated due to instrument drift
- zinc measurements were slightly higher than expected but within detection limits

These adjustments were logged in the internal lab record but are not included in this summary table.

---

## 8. Recommendations

Based on the preliminary findings:

- conduct extended monitoring near Site S05 and S06
- increase sampling density in Zone C
- test for additional pollutants such as arsenic and chromium
- monitor seasonal variation

It may also be useful to analyze sediment movement patterns, as some contamination could be transported downstream rather than originating locally.

---

## 9. Concluding Remarks

The sediment survey suggests that contamination increases significantly in the downstream section of the river basin.

While the data indicate a potential influence from nearby industrial discharge, further sampling and analysis will be required before any regulatory action is recommended.

Additional monitoring during the next quarter should provide a clearer trend analysis.
